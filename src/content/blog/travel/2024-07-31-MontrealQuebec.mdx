---
🏰 Québec City: Fortress of history, cradle of French America
Perched atop Cap Diamant, Québec City commands the narrowing of the St. Lawrence River, a strategic position that made it the capital of New France and later a fortified British stronghold. Its origins stretch back thousands of years, beginning with Indigenous peoples and later the Iroquoian village of Stadacona, visited by Jacques Cartier in 1535. 
Champlain’s 1608 settlement grew into a bustling fur-trading hub, protected by fortifications that still stand today—making Old Québec (Vieux-Québec) the only walled city north of Mexico and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. 
Key historic anchors
• Château Frontenac — the iconic cliff‑top hotel overlooking the river.
• Fortifications & Citadel — remnants of French and British military engineering.
• Place Royale — the birthplace of French civilization in North America.
• Plains of Abraham — site of the pivotal 1759 battle that reshaped the continent.

🌆 Montreal: Where old-world charm meets modern momentum
Montreal pulses with modern energy, sport, and cosmopolitan culture, while Québec City stands as the oldest permanent French settlement in North America, founded in 1608 by Samuel de Champlain. Together, they form the cultural spine of French Canada—one defined by centuries of exploration, conflict, architecture, and artistic evolution.
• Old Montréal mirrors Old Québec with cobblestones, basilicas, and 17th‑century architecture.
• Cycling prestige: Montreal is a UCI Bike City, hosting world‑class races and maintaining one of North America’s most advanced cycling infrastructures.
• Hockey devotion: The Montreal Canadiens remain a cultural institution, their legacy woven into the city’s identity.
