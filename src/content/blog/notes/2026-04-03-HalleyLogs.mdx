## ⭐ Ronald L. Halley Jr. (1934-1999)

**Civil Engineer · U.S. Army Officer · Forest Service Surveyor · International Dam Builder**
**Founder of Hydro Triad, Inc. · Volunteer Ski Patroller · Mountain Safety Instructor**

---

## 🧒 Early Life (1934-1952)

Ronald L. Halley Jr. was born on 20 September 1934 in Richland, Oregon's Eagle Valley, into one of the area's long-established farming families.

He was the eldest son of Ronald Lloyd Halley Sr. and Grace Margaret (Jones) Halley, and grew up working on the Halley family farm.

Early influences included:

- 🚜 Rural engineering — irrigation ditches, farm structures, water systems
- 🏔️ The geography of the Wallowa Mountains and Hells Canyon
- 🔩 A family culture built around hard work, precision, and self-reliance

---

## 🎓 Education (1952-1958)

Ronald Jr. attended Pine Eagle High School, then the University of Washington, earning:

- 📐 Bachelor's in Civil Engineering
- 📐 Master's in Civil Engineering

UW's civil engineering curriculum in the 1950s covered hydrology, structural engineering, surveying, water resource development, and early computational methods — a foundation well-suited to both Forest Service fieldwork and later international dam construction.

---

## 🎖️ Military Service — Fort Bliss, Texas

During the Korean War era, Ronald Jr. served as a U.S. Army officer at Fort Bliss, one of the Army's primary artillery training installations.

Responsibilities included:

- 🪖 Training soldiers in light to medium artillery
- 📋 Leadership, logistics, and technical instruction
- 🏗️ Exposure to large-scale military engineering and infrastructure

The experience reinforced discipline, precision, and the ability to direct large teams under demanding conditions.

---

## 🌲 Forest Service Surveying — Washington State

Following military service, Ronald Jr. joined the U.S. Forest Service, conducting surveys across undeveloped regions of Washington State.

Work during this period included:

- 🗺️ Terrain mapping and road alignment studies
- 💧 Watershed assessments and hydrological planning
- 🧭 Field operations in rugged conditions, well before GPS technology

This work contributed to the groundwork for modern forest roads and water management infrastructure across the Pacific Northwest.

---

## ⛑️ Volunteer Ski Patrol — Loveland Ski Area (30+ Years)

Ronald Jr. served as a volunteer ski patroller at Loveland Ski Area in Colorado for more than three decades, reaching senior ranks within the patrol.

Contributions included:

- 🎿 Assisting injured skiers and executing mountain rescues
- 🛷 Proficiency with toboggan-based rescue techniques and early mountain tools
- ❄️ Teaching avalanche-rescue basics and snow science to newer patrollers
- 🏔️ Training recruits in mountain safety and emergency response protocols

The work was entirely voluntary throughout — driven by a commitment to service and a preference for time in the mountains.

---

## 💍 Marriage & Family (1955-1961)

Ronald Jr. married Margaret Marie Garcia. Together they had four children, born between 1955 and 1961.

During these years he was simultaneously building an early engineering career, raising a family, and taking on increasingly complex professional work.

---

## 🏗️ Engineering Career — E.C.I. & International Dam Work (1960s-1970s)

Ronald Jr. joined E.C.I. (Engineering Consultants Inc.), a U.S. engineering firm active in global infrastructure development during a period when American technical expertise was widely deployed across developing regions.

### 🇹🇭 Thailand: The Sirikit Dam

One of his most significant assignments was in Thailand, contributing to the construction of the Sirikit Dam — at the time the country's largest earthfill dam.

**Project facts:**

- 📏 Height: 113.6 m · Length: 800 m
- 💧 Reservoir capacity: 9.51 billion cubic meters
- 🗓️ Construction: 1968-1972
- ⚡ Purpose: Irrigation, flood control, hydroelectric power

The dam was among the largest embankment dams in Southeast Asia at the time and remains a cornerstone of Thailand's water management infrastructure.

### 🌏 Regional Context

Thailand served as a logistical hub during the Vietnam War era. American engineering firms — E.C.I. among them — were contracted to support the country's infrastructure expansion. The Halley family lived in a U.S. residential compound during the assignment.

### 🤝 Cross-Cultural Engagement

## Ronald Jr. approached new cultures with genuine curiosity and respect. He built strong working relationships with Thai colleagues, neighbors, and local workers throughout the assignment.

## 🏢 Return to the U.S. — Colorado & Hydro Triad, Inc. (1970s-1998)

After returning from Southeast Asia, Ronald Jr. settled in Colorado and founded **Hydro Triad, Inc.**, an international civil engineering firm.

Areas of practice:

- 💧 Hydrology and water resource engineering
- 🏞️ Dam and reservoir design
- 🌿 Environmental and watershed studies
- 🌐 International infrastructure consulting

Hydro Triad developed a global client base over the following decades. Ronald Jr. sold the company in 1998.

---

## ⛵ Sailing & the Caribbean

Ronald Jr. kept a sailboat in the Caribbean and spent extended time at sea throughout his adult life. Sailing combined navigation, self-sufficiency, and time in open water — interests that ran alongside his engineering and mountain work for decades.

---

## 🕊️ Passing & Final Resting Place (1999)

Ronald L. Halley Jr. died on 15 May 1999, shortly after the sale of Hydro Triad.

At his request, family and friends gathered to spread his ashes on the foothills of Cornucopia Mountain in Oregon — a remote area he reached by horse, where he had camped, fished, and spent time throughout his life. He chose no permanent marker; instead, a gathering and a return to the landscapes he valued most.
