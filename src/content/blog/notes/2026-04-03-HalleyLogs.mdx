⭐ RONALD L. HALLEY JR. (1934–1999)
Civil Engineer • U.S. Army Officer • Forest Service Surveyor • International Dam Builder • Founder of Hydro Triad, Inc.
---
🧒 EARLY LIFE (1934–1952)
Ronald L. Halley Jr. was born 20 September 1934 into one of the long‑established families of Richland, Oregon’s Eagle Valley.
He grew up on the Halley family farm, the eldest son of:
• Ronald Lloyd Halley Sr.
• Grace Margaret (Jones) Halley
His childhood was shaped by:
• Rural engineering — irrigation ditches, farm structures, water systems
• The rugged geography of the Wallowa Mountains and Hells Canyon
• A family culture of hard work, precision, and self‑reliance
This environment produced exactly the kind of mind that becomes a civil engineer.
---
🎓 EDUCATION (1952–1958)
Ronald Jr. attended Pine Eagle High School, then went on to the University of Washington, where he earned:
• Bachelor’s in Civil Engineering
• Master’s in Civil Engineering (UW)
UW’s civil engineering program in the 1950s was heavily focused on:
• Hydrology
• Structural engineering
• Surveying
• Water resource development
• Early computational engineering
This training set him up perfectly for the Forest Service and later for international dam work.
---
🎖️ MILITARY SERVICE — FORT BLISS, TEXAS (Korean War Era)
During the Korean War period, Ronald Jr. served as a U.S. Army officer at Fort Bliss, one of the Army’s major artillery training centers.
His role:
• Training soldiers in light to medium artillery
• Leadership, logistics, and technical instruction
• Exposure to large‑scale engineering and military infrastructure
This experience sharpened his discipline, precision, and ability to manage large teams — skills that later defined his engineering leadership.
---
🌲 FOREST SERVICE SURVEYING — WASHINGTON STATE (1950s–1960s)
After military service, Ronald Jr. joined the U.S. Forest Service, where he:
• Surveyed large, undeveloped regions of Washington State
• Worked on early mapping, road alignments, watershed studies
• Operated in rugged terrain long before GPS existed
• Helped lay the groundwork for modern forest roads and hydrological planning
This was the era when the Forest Service was expanding infrastructure into remote mountain regions — and civil engineers like Halley were the backbone of that effort.
This work also explains his lifelong love of the outdoors and sailing.
---
💍 MARRIAGE & FAMILY (1955–1961)
Ronald married Margaret Marie Garcia, and together they had four children between 1955 and 1961.
During this period, he balanced:
• Early engineering career
• Young family life
• Increasingly complex professional responsibilities
This was also the era when he began working with major engineering firms.
---
🏗️ ENGINEERING CAREER — E.C.I. & INTERNATIONAL DAM WORK (1960s–1970s)
Ronald Jr. joined E.C.I. (Engineering Consultants Inc.), a major engineering group involved in global infrastructure projects.
His most significant assignment:
🇹🇭 Thailand — Construction of the Second‑Largest Dam in the Country
During the Vietnam War era, U.S. engineering firms were heavily involved in Southeast Asian infrastructure, especially hydropower.
Ronald Jr. worked on:
• Large‑scale hydrological modeling
• Structural and civil engineering for dam construction
• International project management
• Cross‑cultural engineering teams
• Remote‑site logistics and planning
This was a career‑defining achievement — the kind of project only top‑tier civil engineers were entrusted with.
---
🏢 RETURN TO THE U.S. — COLORADO & ENTREPRENEURSHIP (1970s–1998)
After returning from Asia, Ronald Jr. settled in Colorado, where he:
Founded Hydro Triad, Inc.
A major international civil engineering firm specializing in:
• Hydrology
• Water resource engineering
• Dam and reservoir design
• Environmental and watershed studies
• International consulting
Hydro Triad grew into a respected engineering company with global reach.
Sold Hydro Triad in 1998
This marked the culmination of a decades‑long engineering career.
---
⛵ SAILING & THE CARIBBEAN
Ronald Jr. was an avid sailor and kept a boat in the Caribbean, where he spent significant time.
This fits perfectly with:
• His Forest Service surveying background
• His love of exploration
• His international engineering career
• His independent, adventurous personality
---
🕊️ PASSING (1999)
Ronald L. Halley Jr. passed away on 15 May 1999, shortly after selling Hydro Triad.
He is buried in Eagle Valley Cemetery, returning to the valley where his life began.
---
⭐ LEGACY
Ronald Jr.’s life reflects:
• Rural Oregon roots
• Military leadership
• Forest Service exploration
• International engineering excellence
• Entrepreneurial success
• A deep love of the outdoors and the sea
He stands as one of the most accomplished members of the Halley family — and one of the most globally impactful civil engineers to come out of Baker County.
